## Overview

This guide covers **equipment-based vestibular testing** using:
- **VNG** (Videonystagmography) with infrared goggles
- **RealEyes** BPPV testing system
- **Caloric irrigators** (bithermal testing)

These tests go beyond clinical bedside examination to provide **objective, quantifiable vestibular function assessment**.

---

## VNG - Videonystagmography Complete Assessment

**Code**: `VNG_COMPLETE`
**Duration**: 45 minutes
**Equipment**: VNG goggles with infrared cameras, computer system, rotating chair/stripe patterns

### Test Components (9 Tests)

#### 1. Spontan Nystagmus (Spontaneous Nystagmus)

**Purpose**: Detect nystagmus present without stimulation
**Position**: Sitting, eyes closed or darkness (VNG goggles)
**Duration**: 30-60 seconds

**Scoring**:
- **ua** (uauffallende) = unremarkable, normal ✓
- **Present**: Direction (horizontal/vertical/rotatory), intensity

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Spontan nystagmus: ua

❌ Abnormal:
  Spontan nystagmus: ustø mulig ve rettet (unsteady, possibly left-directed)
  Spontan nystagmus: Hø rettet - pendler (right-directed, pendular)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Horizontal nystagmus**:
  - Peripheral: Beats AWAY from lesion (toward intact side)
  - Suppressed by visual fixation
- **Vertical nystagmus**: Suggests CENTRAL pathology
- **Pendular**: Cerebellar or brainstem pathology
- **Hø (right-directed)**: May indicate left peripheral or right central

---

#### 2. Gaze Horisontalt (Horizontal Gaze)

**Purpose**: Assess gaze-holding ability
**Positions**: 30° left, center, 30° right
**Duration**: 20-30 seconds each position

**Scoring**:
- **ua** = normal stable gaze ✓
- **Drift**: Direction noted (høyre/venstre/diagonal)
- **Jerk**: Gaze-evoked nystagmus
- **Nystagmus**: Present/absent

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Gaze horisontalt: ua

❌ Abnormal patterns:
  Gaze horisontalt: Drift på Hø (drift on right gaze)
  Gaze horisontalt: jerk Hø (jerk nystagmus to right)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Drift**: Gaze instability → cerebellar dysfunction
- **Gaze-evoked nystagmus**: Central pathology, medication effect
- **Diagonal drift**: Combined horizontal + vertical involvement

---

#### 3. Gaze Vertikalt (Vertical Gaze)

**Positions**: 30° up, center, 30° down
**Duration**: 20-30 seconds each

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Gaze vertikalt: ua

❌ Abnormal:
  Gaze vertikalt: Drift Hø opp diagonalt (drift right-upward diagonal)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Upbeat nystagmus**: Cerebellar vermis, medulla
- **Downbeat nystagmus**: Cervicomedullary junction (Arnold-Chiari)
- **Diagonal drift**: Right superior cerebellar/brainstem involvement

---

#### 4. Sakkader Horisontalt (Horizontal Saccades)

**Purpose**: Test rapid eye movements between targets
**Method**: Patient shifts gaze between two targets 30° apart
**Variables**: Increase speed to challenge system

**Scoring**:
- **Accuracy**: Normal / overshoot / undershoot / **catch-up saccades**
- **Velocity**: Normal / slow / asymmetric
- **Side preference**: Høyre slower / Venstre slower
- **Symptoms**: None / dizziness / nausea

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Sakkader horisontalt: ua

❌ Abnormal patterns:
  Sakkader horisontalt: Catch up sakader Spess Hø - ++ fart
  (Catch-up saccades especially right, worse with increased speed)

  Sakkader horisontalt: catch up bi lat
  (Bilateral catch-up saccades)

  Sakkader horisontalt: noe overshoots
  (Some overshoots - hypermetria)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Catch-up saccades**:
  - IPSILATERAL cerebellar hemisphere dysfunction
  - "Spess Hø" = Right cerebellar dysfunction
- **Overshoots**: Hypermetria - cerebellar
- **Undershoots**: Hypometria - cerebellar or fatigue
- **Slowed**: Brainstem pathology, PSP
- **Asymmetric**: Ipsilateral cerebellar hemisphere

---

#### 5. Sakkader Vertikalt (Vertical Saccades)

**Method**: Targets 30° apart vertically
**Challenge**: Increase speed

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Sakkader vertikalt: ua

❌ Abnormal:
  Sakkader vertikalt: Catch up sakader Spess øker ved fart - blir svimmel
  (Catch-up saccades especially increase with speed - becomes dizzy)

  Sakkader vertikalt: Catch up>ned
  (Catch-up saccades more on downward)

  Sakkader vertikalt: catch up opp og ned
  (Catch-up saccades both up and down)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Upward limitation**: Midbrain pathology, PSP
- **Downward limitation**: Dorsal midbrain
- **Catch-up down**: Cerebellar vermis
- **Symptoms with speed**: Vestibular-visual mismatch, compensation limits

---

#### 6. Pursuits Horisontalt (Horizontal Smooth Pursuits)

**Purpose**: Test ability to smoothly track moving target
**Method**: Target moves sinusoidally 0.2-0.4 Hz
**Challenge**: Increase velocity

**Scoring**:
- **Smoothness**: Smooth / saccadic / catch-up saccades
- **Side comparison**: Mildt nedsatt (mildly reduced) left/right
- **Velocity effect**: Deterioration at speed
- **Symptoms**: Dizziness, nausea

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Pursuits horisontalt: ua (smooth tracking both directions)

❌ Abnormal patterns:
  Pursuits horisontalt: mildt nedsatt VE men økende div ved høyre fart
  (Mildly reduced LEFT but increasing divergence/deterioration with right speed)

  Pursuits horisontalt: Hø men økende div ved høyre fart
  (RIGHT reduced with increasing divergence at right speed)

  Pursuits horisontalt: noe overshoots med cacthupsakkader begge retninger
  (Some overshoots with catch-up saccades both directions)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Saccadic pursuits**: Inability to maintain smooth tracking
  - → Cerebellar or brainstem pathology
- **Unilateral deficit**: IPSILATERAL cerebellar hemisphere
- **Bilateral deficit**: Diffuse cerebellar, medication, age-related
- **Velocity-dependent deterioration**: Compensation limits reached
- **"Økende div ved fart"**: System overwhelmed at higher speeds

---

#### 7. Pursuits Vertikalt (Vertical Smooth Pursuits)

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  Pursuits vertikalt: ua

❌ Abnormal:
  Pursuits vertikalt: mildt nedsatt VE men økende div ved høyre fart
  (Mildly reduced LEFT with deterioration at right speed)

  Pursuits vertikalt: mildt nedsatt Hø men økende div ved høyre fart
  (Mildly reduced RIGHT with deterioration at right speed)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Vertical pursuit deficit**: Cerebellar vermis, brainstem
- **Upward deficit**: Dorsal vermis
- **Downward deficit**: Ventral brainstem

---

#### 8. OPK Horisontalt (Optokinetic Nystagmus Horizontal)

**Purpose**: Tests optokinetic reflex with rotating stripes
**Method**: Stripe pattern rotates left and right
**Measurement**: Gain (slow phase velocity / stimulus velocity)

**Scoring**:
- **Gain values**: Left-beating vs. Right-beating
- **Format**: "Hø___/Ve___" (Right/Left velocity ratio)
- **Symmetry**: Symmetric / asymmetric
- **Symptoms**: Dizziness, nausea, chair tipping sensation

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  OPK horisontalt: ua (symmetric gain ~0.3-0.6)

❌ Abnormal patterns:
  OPK horisontalt: Hø - blir svimmel og kvalm
  (Right direction - becomes dizzy and nauseous)

  OPK horisontalt: gain Ve: Hø98/Ve82 Hø:Hø75/Ve107
  (Left-beating: R98/L82, Right-beating: R75/L107)
  Asymmetric - Left vestibular system weaker

  OPK horisontalt: Svimmel - føler at stolen tipper
  (Dizzy - feels that chair is tipping)
  → Otolith dysfunction / spatial orientation disturbance
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Normal gain**: 0.3-0.6
- **>25% asymmetry**: Unilateral peripheral vestibular or central pathology
- **Reduced bilateral**: Bilateral vestibular loss, cerebellar disease
- **Symptoms høyre**: Right-sided vestibular/cerebellar dysfunction
- **Chair tipping**: Otolith dysfunction, spatial disorientation

**Gain Calculation**:
```
Ve: Hø98/Ve82 means:
  Left-beating OPK: Right eye 98°/s, Left eye 82°/s

Hø: Hø75/Ve107 means:
  Right-beating OPK: Right eye 75°/s, Left eye 107°/s

Interpretation: Left eye has higher velocity during right-beating
→ Suggests left vestibular system compensation or right weakness
```

---

#### 9. OPK Vertikalt (Optokinetic Nystagmus Vertical)

**Method**: Vertical stripe movement (up and down)

**Clinical Examples**:
```
✓ Normal:
  OPK vertikalt: ua

❌ Abnormal patterns:
  OPK vertikalt: Ned svimmel - Opp Kvalm og svimmel + gain - opp slår Hø mot Ve nyst
  (Down dizzy - Up nauseous and dizzy + increased gain - upward beats Right toward Left nystagmus)

  OPK vertikalt: blir sliten opp - bedre ned - spess Hø
  (Becomes tired upward - better downward - especially Right)
```

**Interpretation**:
- **Upward more difficult**: Normal - upward OPK more demanding
- **Upward deficit**: Dorsal brainstem/midbrain pathology
- **Downward better**: Common pattern
- **Fatigue upward**: Central vestibular compensation limits
- **Horizontal component during vertical**: Combined pathway involvement
- **"Slår Hø mot Ve"**: Beats from right toward left (horizontal component)

## VNG Interpretation Patterns

### Peripheral Vestibular Pattern
```
✓ Spontaneous horizontal nystagmus (away from lesion)
✓ Suppressed by visual fixation
✓ Normal saccades and pursuits
✓ Asymmetric OPK gain
✗ No vertical nystagmus
✗ No catch-up saccades
```

**Example**:
```
Spontan nystagmus: Hø rettet (right-beating → left peripheral weakness)
Gaze horisontalt: ua
Sakkader: ua
Pursuits: ua
OPK horisontalt: Asymmetric gain favoring right
```

---

### Central Cerebellar Pattern
```
✓ Catch-up saccades (ipsilateral hemisphere)
✓ Saccadic pursuits
✓ Gaze-evoked nystagmus
✓ Vertical nystagmus possible
✓ Velocity-dependent deterioration
```

**Example**:
```
Spontan nystagmus: ua or mild
Gaze: Drift or gaze-evoked nystagmus
Sakkader horisontalt: Catch up sakader Spess Hø
Pursuits: Mildt nedsatt, økende div ved fart
OPK: May show asymmetry
```

---

### Brainstem Pattern
```
✓ Slowed saccades
✓ Vertical gaze limitation
✓ Skew deviation
✓ Internuclear ophthalmoplegia (INO)
✓ Vertical nystagmus
```

---

### Bilateral Vestibular Loss
```
✓ No spontaneous nystagmus
✓ Symmetric reduced/absent OPK gain
✓ Oscillopsia with head movement
✓ Normal saccades and pursuits (central intact)
```

## BPPV Testing with RealEyes Equipment

**Code**: `BPPV_REALEYES`
**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Equipment**: RealEyes infrared video goggles

### Advantages of RealEyes
- ✅ **Infrared vision**: Eliminates visual fixation suppression
- ✅ **Video recording**: Document nystagmus for review
- ✅ **Superior sensitivity**: Detects subtle nystagmus
- ✅ **Patient education**: Show patient their own nystagmus

---

### Test 1: Dix-Hallpike (Posterior Canal BPPV)

**Clinical Examples from Your Data**:

```
BPPV test med real eyes:
Dix Hallpike: Hø geo torsjon
(Right geotorsional nystagmus)

Interpretation:
✓ Right posterior canal BPPV - Canalithiasis
✓ Geotorsional (geotropic torsional) pattern
✓ Expected: Latency 2-30s, Duration <60s, Reversal on sitting
✓ Treatment: Epley maneuver høyre side
```

**Canalithiasis vs. Cupulolithiasis**:

| Feature | Canalithiasis | Cupulolithiasis |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Latency** | 2-30 seconds | None (immediate) |
| **Duration** | <60 seconds | Sustained (as long as position held) |
| **Nystagmus** | Up-beating torsional | Down-beating or atypical |
| **Reversal** | Present | Absent |
| **Treatment** | Epley standard | Modified Epley/Semont |
| **Frequency** | 90% of BPPV | 10% of BPPV |

**Treatment Prescription**:
```
Right Posterior Canal BPPV - Canalithiasis:
✓ Epley's Maneuver Høyre
✓ Duration: 1 minute each position
✓ Frequency: 2x daglig (2x daily)
✓ Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aC7x161MHhU
✓ Precautions: Sleep elevated 45° first night
```

---

### Test 2: Supine Roll / Pagnini-McClure (Lateral Canal BPPV)

**Clinical Examples**:
```
Supine rolle test: hø hor
(Right horizontal nystagmus)

Interpretation:
✓ Right lateral (horizontal) canal BPPV
✓ Geotropic pattern (beats toward ground)
✓ Affected ear = side with STRONGER nystagmus
✓ Treatment: BBQ Roll høyre
```

**Geotropic vs. Apogeotropic**:

| Pattern | Nystagmus Direction | Affected Ear | Pathology |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Geotropic** | Toward ground | Stronger response | Canalithiasis (debris floating) |
| **Apogeotropic** | Away from ground | Weaker response | Cupulolithiasis (debris stuck to cupula) |

**Treatment Prescription**:
```
Right Lateral Canal BPPV - Geotropic:
✓ BBQ Roll Venstre (rotate TOWARD unaffected ear)
✓ OR Gufoni maneuver
✓ Duration: 30 seconds each position
✓ Frequency: 2x daglig
✓ Video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KNBOASk7Ny8
```

---

### Test 3: Foroverbøy / Deep Head Hanging (Anterior Canal)

**Anterior Canal BPPV** (Rare: <1% of BPPV cases)

**Findings**:
- Down-beating nystagmus in head hanging position
- OR up-beating in bow (forward flexion) position

**Treatment**:
- Deep head hanging maneuver
- Reverse Epley
- Liberatory maneuver
- **Often requires specialist referral** - challenging to treat

## Caloric Testing - Bithermal Protocol

**Code**: `CALORIC_BITHERMAL`
**Duration**: 60 minutes (5-7 min intervals between irrigations)
**Equipment**: Caloric irrigator, VNG goggles, water at 44°C and 30°C

### COWS Mnemonic

**"Cold Opposite, Warm Same"**

Describes expected nystagmus direction:
- **Cold water** → Nystagmus beats OPPOSITE (away from) irrigated ear
- **Warm water** → Nystagmus beats SAME (toward) irrigated ear

### Test Sequence (Standard Bithermal)

1. **Right Warm (44°C)** → Expect LEFT-beating nystagmus
2. **Left Warm (44°C)** → Expect RIGHT-beating nystagmus
3. **Right Cold (30°C)** → Expect RIGHT-beating nystagmus
4. **Left Cold (30°C)** → Expect LEFT-beating nystagmus

**Wait 5-7 minutes between each** to allow nystagmus to resolve.

---

### Measurements

**For each irrigation**:
- Peak slow phase velocity (SPV) in degrees/second
- Duration of nystagmus
- Vertigo intensity (mild/moderate/severe)
- Nausea/vomiting

**Normal Range**: 15-60 deg/sec peak velocity

---

### Jongkees Formulas

#### 1. Unilateral Weakness (UW)

**Formula**:
```
UW = [(RC + RW) - (LC + LW)] / (RC + RW + LC + LW) × 100%
```

**Where**:
- RC = Right Cold peak velocity
- RW = Right Warm peak velocity
- LC = Left Cold peak velocity
- LW = Left Warm peak velocity

**Interpretation**:
- **<25%**: Normal (symmetric function)
- **25-50%**: Significant unilateral weakness
- **>50%**: Marked unilateral weakness
- **>90%**: Near-complete or complete unilateral loss

**Example**:
```
Right Warm: 45 deg/sec
Left Warm: 40 deg/sec
Right Cold: 42 deg/sec
Left Cold: 15 deg/sec (reduced!)

UW = [(42 + 45) - (15 + 40)] / (42 + 45 + 15 + 40) × 100
   = [87 - 55] / 142 × 100
   = 32 / 142 × 100
   = 22.5%

Interpretation: Borderline normal, but LEFT ear shows reduced response
Clinical correlation needed - may be early Meniere's, vestibular neuritis recovery
```

---

#### 2. Directional Preponderance (DP)

**Formula**:
```
DP = [(RW + LC) - (LW + RC)] / (RW + LC + LW + RC) × 100%
```

**Interpretation**:
- **<30%**: Normal
- **≥30%**: Significant directional preponderance
  - Suggests central imbalance or incomplete peripheral compensation

---

### Clinical Interpretation

#### Pattern 1: Vestibular Neuritis
```
Right UW: 75% (marked reduction right ear)
DP: 10% (normal)

Interpretation: Acute right vestibular neuritis
- Ipsilateral peripheral vestibular loss
- Central compensation beginning (normal DP)
```

#### Pattern 2: Meniere's Disease
```
Left UW: 40% (moderate reduction)
DP: 25% (normal)
Fluctuating over time

Interpretation: Meniere's disease left ear
- Variable unilateral weakness
- May normalize between episodes
```

#### Pattern 3: Bilateral Vestibulopathy
```
All responses: <10 deg/sec
UW: 5% (symmetric)
DP: 8% (normal)

Interpretation: Bilateral vestibular loss
- Gentamicin toxicity
- Bilateral Meniere's
- Autoimmune inner ear disease
```

#### Pattern 4: Central Lesion
```
UW: 15% (normal)
DP: 45% (abnormal)
Paradoxical or atypical responses

Interpretation: Central vestibular pathology
- Brainstem lesion
- Cerebellar stroke
- Requires neuroimaging
```

## Home Exercise Prescriptions

### Epley Maneuver (Posterior Canal BPPV)

**Video**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aC7x161MHhU

**Instructions**:
```
1. Sit on edge of bed
2. Turn head 45° toward affected ear
3. Rapidly recline to head hanging 20° below horizontal
4. Hold 1 minute
5. Turn head 90° toward opposite side
6. Hold 1 minute
7. Roll onto side (nose pointing down 45°)
8. Hold 1 minute
9. Sit up slowly

Frequency: 2x daglig (2x daily)
Duration: Continue until symptom-free for 24 hours
Precautions: Sleep elevated 45° first night, avoid provocative head positions 48h
```

---

### BBQ Roll (Lateral Canal BPPV)

**Video**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KNBOASk7Ny8

**Instructions**:
```
360° log roll AWAY from affected ear:

1. Lie supine
2. Roll 90° onto UNAFFECTED side
3. Hold 30 seconds
4. Roll another 90° (face down)
5. Hold 30 seconds
6. Roll another 90° (onto affected side)
7. Hold 30 seconds
8. Roll final 90° back to supine
9. Sit up

Frequency: 2x daglig
Alternative: Gufoni maneuver (single-position treatment)
```

## Clinical Workflow Integration

### Dizziness Assessment Protocol

**Step 1: History**
- Vertigo (spinning) vs. Lightheadedness vs. Imbalance
- Positional? → BPPV likely
- Constant? → Peripheral or central vestibular
- Episodic? → Meniere's, migraine, TIA

**Step 2: Initial Testing**
- Pre-Adjustment Safety Screen (BP, Romberg, VBI)
- Vestibular Mastersheet (bedside cerebellar + VOR)

**Step 3: Equipment-Based Testing** (if available)
- Suspected BPPV → RealEyes BPPV Battery
- Suspected peripheral weakness → Caloric Testing
- Suspected central pathology → Complete VNG Battery

**Step 4: Treatment**
- BPPV → Repositioning maneuvers (Epley, BBQ roll)
- Peripheral hypofunction → VOR adaptation exercises
- Central pathology → Referral to neurology + supportive care
