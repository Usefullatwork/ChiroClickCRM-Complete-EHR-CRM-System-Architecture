## Knee Assessment Batteries

### 1. ACL Injury Assessment Battery (`ACL_BATTERY`)

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Target**: Athletes, acute knee injury, suspected ACL tear

**Tests Included**:
1. **Lachman Test** (Gold Standard)
   - Sensitivity: 85% | Specificity: 94%
   - Grade translation: 0/I/II/III
   - Endpoint quality: Firm/Soft

2. **Lever Sign Test**
   - Sensitivity: 100% | Specificity: 100%
   - Most accurate when patient relaxed
   - Heel rise = negative (ACL intact)

3. **Anterior Drawer Test**
   - Sensitivity: 62% | Specificity: 67%
   - Supplementary to Lachman

4. **Pivot Shift Test**
   - Sensitivity: 24% | Specificity: 98%
   - Highly specific for rotatory instability
   - Grade 0/I/II/III

5. **Knee Effusion Tests**
   - Sweep test (small effusion)
   - Ballottement (large effusion)

**Scoring**: Count positive tests
- **0-1 positive**: Low probability ACL tear
- **2 positive**: Moderate probability ACL tear
- **≥3 positive**: High probability - MRI indicated

**Clinical Pearl**: ≥3 positive tests = 95% sensitivity, 94% specificity for ACL tear

---

### 2. PCL Injury Assessment Battery (`PCL_BATTERY`)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**Target**: Dashboard injury, hyperflexion trauma, posterior knee pain

**Tests Included**:
1. **Posterior Sag Sign** (Gravity Drawer)
   - Sensitivity: 79% | Specificity: 100%
   - Patient supine, knee 90°, observe tibial step-off

2. **Posterior Drawer Test**
   - Sensitivity: 90% | Specificity: 99%
   - Grade 0/I/II/III translation

3. **Dial Test**
   - >10° asymmetry at 30° only → PLC injury
   - >10° at both 30° and 90° → Combined PCL + PLC

4. **Quadriceps Active Test**
   - Anterior tibial translation with quad contraction = PCL tear

**Scoring**:
- **0**: PCL intact
- **1**: Possible PCL injury
- **≥2**: High probability PCL tear

---

### 3. Meniscal Tear Assessment Battery (`MENISCUS_BATTERY`)

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Target**: Mechanical symptoms, joint line pain, locking/catching

**Tests Included**:
1. **Joint Line Tenderness**
   - Sensitivity: 83% | Specificity: 50%
   - Most sensitive single test
   - Assess medial and lateral separately

2. **Thessaly Test** ⭐ HIGHEST ACCURACY
   - Sensitivity: 89% | Specificity: 97%
   - 20° knee flexion, rotate on planted foot
   - Note pain location, click, locking sensation

3. **McMurray Test**
   - Sensitivity: 61% | Specificity: 84%
   - IR-extension for medial meniscus
   - ER-extension for lateral meniscus

4. **Apley Compression Test**
   - Pain with compression → meniscus
   - Pain with distraction → ligament

5. **Ege Test** (Squat Test)
   - Deep squat with rotation
   - Medial pain = medial meniscus
   - Lateral pain = lateral meniscus

**Scoring**:
- **0-1 positive**: Low probability
- **2-3 positive**: Moderate probability - consider imaging
- **4-5 positive**: High probability - MRI indicated

**Clinical Pearl**: Joint line tenderness + Thessaly positive = 97% specificity

## Vestibular & Cerebellar Assessment Batteries

### 4. Vestibular Mastersheet (`VESTIBULAR_MASTERSHEET`)

**Duration**: 25 minutes
**Target**: Dizziness, vertigo, balance disorders, post-concussion, cerebellar dysfunction

Based on functional neurology approach to hemispheric balance.

**Tests Included**:

#### Balance & Cerebellar Tests (IPSILATERAL)

1. **One Leg Standing Balance**
   - **Normal**: 30 sec eyes open, 10 sec eyes closed
   - **Abnormal**: Imbalance indicates IPSILATERAL cerebellar dysfunction
   - Test both legs

2. **Romberg 7-Step Test**
   - Firm surface: eyes open/closed
   - Foam pad: eyes open/closed
   - **Interpretation**:
     - Vestibular: Unstable on foam with eyes closed
     - Cerebellar: Unstable even eyes open
     - Proprioceptive: Only unstable eyes closed

3. **Finger to Nose (IPSILATERAL)**
   - **Normal**: Smooth, accurate movement
   - **Cerebellar**: Dysmetria, past-pointing, intention tremor
   - Affects SAME SIDE as cerebellar hemisphere dysfunction

4. **Finger to Finger V2 - Fluid Motion**
   - Patient touches examiner's moving finger
   - **Normal**: Similar speed, no grimacing, no lag
   - **Abnormal**: Different speed, compensatory movements

5. **Rapid Alternating Movements** (Supination Test)
   - **Normal**: Similar speed both sides (dominant slightly faster)
   - **Dysdiadochokinesia**: Irregular, unequal speed, grimacing

6. **Dual Tasking** (Alphabet + Finger Movement)
   - Assesses cognitive-motor integration
   - **Normal**: Maintains both tasks
   - **Abnormal**: Motor or cognitive task deteriorates

7. **Finger Tapping**
   - Rate per 10 seconds
   - Assess rhythm, amplitude consistency

#### Vestibular-Ocular Tests

8. **VOR Test - Head Impulse (HIT)**
   - Tests all 6 semicircular canals
   - **Normal**: Eyes remain fixed on target
   - **Peripheral vestibular**: Catch-up saccade
   - Tests left/right horizontal, anterior/posterior

9. **Band Saccades** (Eye Movements)
   - **Normal**: Quick, accurate saccades
   - **Cerebellar**: Dysmetric (overshoot/undershoot)
   - **Brainstem**: Slowed or absent

**Key Neurophysiology**:
- **90% of cortical output** → ipsilateral brainstem (PMRF)
- **PMRF functions**: Inhibits SNS, inhibits pain, activates extensors, inhibits flexors
- **Head tilt rule**: Often AWAY from dysfunctional cerebellum
  - Right head tilt → suspect LEFT cerebellar dysfunction

**Hemispheric Postural Pattern** (same side):
- Head tilt
- Internal rotation shoulder
- Flexed fingers
- Increased tone
- Elevated ribs
- Reduced costa12-ilium distance
- Externally rotated foot

### 5. BPPV Diagnostic Battery (`BPPV_BATTERY`)

**Duration**: 15 minutes
**Target**: Positional vertigo, brief episodes with head movement

**Tests Included**:

1. **Dix-Hallpike Test** (Right & Left)

   **Canalithiasis Response**:
   - Latency: 2-30 seconds
   - Duration: <30 seconds
   - Nystagmus: Up-beating torsional
   - Reversal: Present
   - **Treatment**: Epley maneuver

   **Cupulolithiasis Response**:
   - No latency
   - Duration: As long as position held
   - No reversal
   - **Treatment**: Modified Epley or Semont

2. **Supine Roll Test** (Lateral Canal)

   **Geotropic Nystagmus**:
   - Canalithiasis
   - Affected ear = stronger nystagmus side
   - **Treatment**: Barbecue roll or Gufoni

   **Apogeotropic Nystagmus**:
   - Cupulolithiasis
   - Affected ear = weaker nystagmus side
   - **Treatment**: Head-shaking, liberatory maneuver

**Clinical Decision Tree**:
```
Positive Dix-Hallpike → Posterior Canal BPPV
  ├─ Canalithiasis pattern → Epley maneuver
  └─ Cupulolithiasis pattern → Modified Epley/Semont

Positive Supine Roll → Lateral Canal BPPV
  ├─ Geotropic → Barbecue roll/Gufoni
  └─ Apogeotropic → Head-shaking/Liberatory
```

### 6. Pre-Adjustment Cervical Safety Screen (`PRE_ADJUST_SCREEN`)

**Duration**: 10 minutes
**REQUIRED**: Before ALL cervical manipulations

**Tests Included**:

1. **Blood Pressure Measurement**
   - **Normal**: SBP <140, DBP <90
   - **Elevated**: SBP 140-159 (caution)
   - **High**: SBP ≥160 → CONTRAINDICATION to HVLA

2. **Romberg Test - Hemispheric Assessment**
   - **Clinical Use**: Determines which side to adjust
   - Eyes open vs. eyes closed
   - Note sway direction → guides treatment side

3. **Vertebrobasilar Insufficiency Screen** ⚠️ CRITICAL
   - Extension-rotation both sides
   - Hold 30-45 seconds
   - **Positive** (dizziness, nystagmus, visual changes, drop attacks):
     - = ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATION to cervical HVLA
   - **Negative**: Safe to proceed

4. **Active Cervical ROM Screening**
   - Assess willingness to move
   - Severe restriction/apprehension → possible instability

5. **Red Flags Checklist**
   - Severe trauma
   - Progressive neurological symptoms
   - Systemically unwell
   - Unexplained weight loss
   - Cancer history
   - Long-term steroids
   - Drop attacks
   - Bilateral upper extremity symptoms

**Pass/Fail Decision**:
- **PASS**: All negative, BP controlled, no red flags → Safe for manipulation
- **FAIL**: Any positive → Use alternative treatment or refer

**Clinical Protocol**:
```
1. Always do Pre-Adjustment Screen FIRST
2. Check BP
3. Perform Romberg → determines treatment side
4. VBI test → MUST be negative
5. If all clear → proceed with adjustment
6. If ANY concerns → modify approach or refer
```

### 7. Hemispheric Balance & Posture Assessment (`HEMISPHERIC_BALANCE`)

**Duration**: 20 minutes
**Target**: Functional neurology assessment, chronic pain, postural dysfunction

**Neurophysiology Foundation**:
- **90% of cortex output** → ipsilateral PMRF
- **Frontal Lobe Areas**:
  - Primary Motor: Execute movements
  - Supplementary Motor: Plan movements
  - Frontal Eye Field: Initiates saccades
  - Prefrontal: Executive control, memory

- **PMRF Functions**:
  - Inhibits: SNS, Pain, Flexors
  - Activates: Extensors (via vestibulospinal tract)

**Tests Included**:

1. **Neurological Posture Assessment**
   - Head tilt/rotation
   - Facial asymmetry
   - Pupil asymmetry
   - Shoulder height & internal rotation
   - Elbow/wrist angle
   - Finger posture (flexed vs. extended)
   - Hip position
   - Costa12-ilium distance (measure both sides)
   - Foot rotation

   **Hemispheric Pattern** (same side as dysfunction):
   - Head tilt (often AWAY from cerebellum)
   - Internal rotated shoulder
   - Flexed fingers
   - Increased tone/muscle activation
   - Elevated ribs (costa12-ilium reduced)
   - Externally rotated foot

2. **Sensory Asymmetry Testing**
   - Hearing (left vs. right)
   - Smell (left vs. right nostril)
   - Touch sensation (face, arms)

3. **Comparative Muscle Strength**
   - Deltoid, biceps, grip
   - Quadriceps, gluteus medius
   - >1 grade asymmetry = significant

4. **PMRF Output Assessment**
   - Extensor vs. flexor tone
   - Ipsilateral effects:
     - Inhibits SNS below T6
     - Inhibits anterior compartment above T6
     - Inhibits posterior compartment below T6

**Clinical Application**:
- Use postural clustering to identify hemispheric dominance
- Guide treatment decisions based on asymmetries
- Right head tilt → suspect LEFT cerebellar dysfunction
- Confirm with cerebellar coordination tests

## Clinical Decision Making

### Knee Examination Protocol

**Step 1: History**
- Mechanism of injury
- Immediate effusion (<2 hours) → ACL, PCL, or fracture
- Delayed effusion (24+ hours) → meniscus likely

**Step 2: Select Battery**
- Acute trauma + instability → ACL Battery
- Dashboard injury → PCL Battery
- Mechanical symptoms → Meniscus Battery

**Step 3: Interpret Results**
- ≥3 positive tests → High probability
- Consider imaging if moderate-high probability

---

### Vestibular Examination Protocol

**Step 1: Chief Complaint Classification**
- Vertigo (spinning) → BPPV Battery + Vestibular Mastersheet
- Imbalance → Romberg, one-leg stand, cerebellar tests
- Post-concussion → Full Vestibular Mastersheet

**Step 2: Differential Diagnosis**
- Positive Dix-Hallpike → BPPV
- Positive VOR/HIT → Peripheral vestibular
- Cerebellar signs → Central pathology
- Normal VOR + balance issues → Proprioceptive/visual

**Step 3: Treatment Planning**
- BPPV → Repositioning maneuvers
- Vestibular hypofunction → VOR exercises
- Cerebellar → Coordination training, refer if severe

---

### Pre-Adjustment Protocol

**ALWAYS perform before cervical manipulation**:

1. ✅ Blood pressure
2. ✅ Romberg (determines side to adjust)
3. ✅ VBI test (MUST be negative)
4. ✅ Red flags checklist
5. ✅ Active ROM

**If all clear** → Proceed with adjustment
**If ANY concern** → Modify or refer
